# Customer Shopping Trends – Target Corporation (U.S.)


---

## Problem
Target stores show inconsistent sales:
- Some stores run out of popular items, others have excess stock.
- Lack of clarity on customer preferences: gender, season, colors.
- Limited insight into loyal customer behavior and spending.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Customer Base
- Total unique customers: **3,900**  
- Gender: **68% Male, 32% Female** → Stock should focus more on male-preferred items, but female preferences remain important.

### 2. Seasonal Trends & Stocking
- Seasons: Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall  
- Top items by season:
  - Fall: Jackets, Hats, Handbags, Skirts
  - Winter: Sunglasses, Pants, Shirts
  - Spring: Sweaters, Shorts, Blouses
  - Summer: Pants, Dresses, Jewelry
- **Insight:** Stock according to season and category demand.

### 3. Location-Based Stocking
- Example: Montana (Fall) → Handbags, Sweaters, T-shirts  
- **Insight:** Each store should have tailored inventory based on local trends.

### 4. Top Customer Experience Locations
- Texas and Wisconsin → Highest average ratings (>3.88)  
- **Insight:** Other stores can adopt best practices from these high-performing locations.

### 5. Loyal Customers
- Customers with ≥10 previous purchases consistently spend more  
- **Insight:** Loyalty programs and targeted campaigns should focus on this group.

---

## Strategic Recommendations
- Adjust inventory by **gender, season, and location**.  
- Promote **top-selling items and colors** for each season.  
- **Replicate best practices** from high-rated stores.  
- **Engage loyal customers** with personalized campaigns to maximize revenue.

---

### One-Line Strategic Insight
*“By analyzing 3,900 customers, we identified top items, seasonal trends, and high-value customers to optimize stock, improve store performance, and drive revenue through targeted strategies.”*
